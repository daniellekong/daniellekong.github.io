Empowering Individuals to Discover Impactful Volunteer Opportunities

User Experience/Product: Design | Research

How can we empower individuals to discover impactful volunteering experiences? How can we make finding volunteer opportunities easier and less complex? In this project, I researched and designed the user experience of a mobile application to connect people with volunteer opportunities. Hence, I named this app Connect.

This project involved defining and branding the product, interviewing individuals from the target user demographic, designing the UX flow, and developing wireframes and prototypes. I used Adobe Illustrator and Figma.

Foreign Domestic Workers in Hong Kong

Multimedia Story: Design | Photography | Research

In the summer of 2015, I received a grant to work on a self-initiated project that involved interviewing, writing, and photographing migrant workers in Hong Kong. I was interested in learning about the lives of foreign domestic workers -- their daily activities, motivations for leaving home countries, and use of urban spaces. The project in 2015 resulted in a photo article. Tufts also published a couple of my short writing pieces about the fieldwork experience and finding common ground .

More recently, I wanted to create an interactive/multimedia web piece with the learnings and photos from this experience. I designed the flow of the story and brought it to life with HTML, CSS, and JS.

Socioeconomic Disparities in Health Outcomes

Visualization: Data | Research | Design

After reading articles about socioeconomic disparities in healthcare access and health outcomes, I became interested in exploring public health datasets. Simultaneously, I was interested in building my skills in web-based data visualization. Thus, this project began!

The aim of this visualization is to convey a relationship between health outcome and socioeconomic status. I used adult diabetes prevalence as an indicator of health outcome and average income as an indicator of socioeconomic status. Data was pulled from the CDC and American Community Survey. The visualization was brought to life with Chart.js.
