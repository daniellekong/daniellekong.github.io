Empowering Individuals to Discover Impactful Volunteer Opportunities

Product Design | User Experience Research

How can we empower individuals to discover impactful volunteering experiences? How can we make finding volunteer opportunities easier and less complex? In this project, I researched and designed the user experience of a mobile application to connect people with volunteer opportunities. Hence, this app was named Connect.

This project involved defining and branding the product, interviewing individuals from the target user demographic, designing the UX flow, and developing wireframes and prototypes. I used Adobe Illustrator and Figma.

Designing Technology and Educational Programs to Teach Children STEM

User Research | Program Design | Marketing

At Tufts, I worked as a research assistant at the DevTech Research Group. With a small team, I researched developmentally appropriate STEM concepts and developed user requirements for an app to teach young kids bioengineering concepts. We observed how kids interacted with prototypes of the app, formulated survey questions, and interviewed the study participants. I enjoyed working in an academic research setting, being part of a team that designs technology to teach STEM.

To complement this experience, I wanted to work in an industry setting to design tech education programs. This led me to a job in Singapore, working at a small start-up called Tinkertanker. I worked with one of the firm's cofounders to help design and launch a new tech education program for their business. I carried out market research to inform curriculum offerings, ran online advertisements, established partnerships with well-known local bloggers for publicity, and worked with the in-house designer to create an online presence for the program. Additionally, I went into classrooms to teach computational thinking and programming concepts.

Foreign Domestic Workers in Hong Kong

Multimedia Design | Photography | Ethnographic Research

In the summer of 2015, I received a grant to work on a self-initiated project that involved interviewing, writing, and photographing migrant workers in Hong Kong. I was interested in learning about the lives of foreign domestic workers -- their daily activities, motivations for leaving home countries, and use of urban spaces. The project in 2015 resulted in a photo article. Tufts also published a couple of my short writing pieces about the fieldwork experience and finding common ground .

More recently, I wanted to create an interactive/multimedia web piece with the learnings and photos from this experience. I designed the flow of the story and brought it to life with HTML, CSS, and JS.
